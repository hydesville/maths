Graphs
(5 hours)

Generate coordinate pairs that satisfy a simple linear function, and relate to real situations.

Construct linear functions arising from real situations.
Understand, construct, and use conversion graphs (currency, imperial/metric etc).
Discuss and interpret graphs arising from real situations such as simple distance-time graphs.

Handling Data
(6 hours)
  

Discuss a problem to be addressed by statistical methods.
Decide on data, sources, sample size, method of collection.
Construct frequency tables, with correct class intervals for continuous data.
Calculate appropriate statistics and graphical representations for data.
Interpret statistics/diagrams and draw inferences relating to the problem.
Compare two distributions using the statistics calculated.
Communicate orally and on paper the results of the statistical enquiry.

 Design, trial, and if necessary refine data sheet.
