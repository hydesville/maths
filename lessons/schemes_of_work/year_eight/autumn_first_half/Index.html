Integers, Powers, Roots
(6 hours)

 Understand and use multiples, factors, primes, squares, cubes.
Order, add, and subtract negative numbers in context.

Add, subtract, multiply, and divide integers, including negatives.
Understand multiples/factors/h.c.f./ l.c.m.
Find prime factor decomposition.
Use index notation (+ve int.) square/cube roots.

number > integers

 Estimate square and cube roots.
Use the prime factor decomposition of a number to find h.c.f./l.c.m.
Use index notation for positive integer powers and use simple cases of the index laws.
Sequences and Functions
(3 hours)
 Generate terms of a simple sequence given a rule.

Generate and describe simple sequences given term to term or position to term definitions, using a spreadsheet or graphical calculator.
Find the nth term of an arithmetic sequence, and link to practical activity.

algebra > sequences

 Extend finding the nth term to include when the difference between successive terms is negative.
Generate quadratic sequences and examine the patterns in differences between successive terms.
Angles
(4 hours)

Identify parallel and perpendicular lines.
Know the sum of angles round a point, on a line and in a triangle, and recognise vertically opposite angles.
Understand acute, obtuse, reflex angles.

Identify alternate and corresponding angles, and know that they are equal.
Understand a proof that angles in a triangle/quadrilateral sum to 180/360?.
Solve a range of geometrical problems using side/angle properties of triangles/quadrilaterals.

shape space > angles

shape space > properties of shapes

 Explain how to find, calculate and use the sums of interior/exterior angles of quadrilaterals, pentagons, and hexagons, and the interior/exterior of regular polygons.
Know the definition of a circle and the names of it’s parts.
Construction
(4 hours)

Measure and draw angles(inc. reflex) to the nearest degree.
Construct triangles using ruler and protractor given 2 sides and the included angle or 2 angles and the included side.

Use straight edge and compasses to construct a triangle given 3 sides, the mid-point and perpendicular bisector of a line, the bisector of an angle, the perpendicular from a point to a line and from a point on a line.

shape space > loci and construction

Use straight edge and compasses to construct a right-angled triangle given the hypotenuse and one other side.
Probability
(5 hours)
 Use the vocabulary and ideas of probability.
Use probability scale from 0 to 1.
Find probability based on equally likely outcomes.
Identify all possible mutually exclusive outcomes of a single event.

Use the vocabulary of probability and appreciate that random processes are unpredictable.
Understand that if the probability of A is p then the probability of not A is 1-p.
Find and record all possible mutually exclusive outcomes for two successive events in a systematic way using diagrams and tables.
Estimate probabilities from experimental data.
Understand that repeating the experiment may result in different outcomes and that increasing the number of repetitions of the experiment generally leads to a better estimate of the probability.

handling data > probability

 Identify all the mutually exclusive outcomes of an experiment, and know that the sum of their probabilities is 1.
Compare experimental and theoretical probabilities in a range of contexts.
Appreciate the difference between mathematical explanation and experimental evidence.
