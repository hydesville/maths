(5 hours)

Read scales to a certain degree of accuracy.
Measure and draw lines to the nearest millimetre.
Understand that area is measured in cm2 .
Understand, measure and calculate perimeters of rectangles and regular polygons.

Know and use formulae for perimeter and area of rectangles and shapes made from rectangles.
Calculate the surface area of cuboids
Make 2D representations of 3D shapes
Understand names/abbrev. of units of area/perimeter and use to measure, estimate, calculate with.

shape space > area and perimeter

 Deduce and use formulae for area of triangle/parallelogram/trapezium.
Know and use the formula for volume of a cuboid.
Expressions and
Formulae
(6 hours)
 Understand the relationships between operational laws.
(eg 3n can be thought of as 3xn or nx3 or ‘3 lots of n’ or n+n+n)
Use brackets to change the order of operations.

Extend understanding of using letters to represent unknown numbers and variables.
Understand that algebraic operations follow the same convention as arithmetic operations.
Simplify linear expressions by collecting like terms and multiplying out a single bracket.
Substitute positive integers into simple formulae.

algebra > algebraic manipulation

 Know the meaning of the words formula, expression, and function.
Use index notation for small positive integer powers.
Substitute into expressions involving small powers.

Angles
(4 hours)

Recognise properties of rectangles.
Use side/angle/symmetry properties to classify triangles.

Identify parallel and perpendicular lines.
Know the sum of angles round a point, on a line and in a triangle, and recognise vertically opposite angles.
Understand how angles, sides, and polygons are labelled.
Understand acute, obtuse, reflex angles.

shape space > angles

 Identify alternate and corresponding angles, and know that they are equal.
Understand a proof that angles in a triangle/quadrilateral sum to 180/360?
Rounding, Order of Operations, Calculations
(6 hours)

Know multiplication facts upto 10x10.
Multiply a 2 digit number by a 2 digit.
Understand the arithmetic laws:
Commutative/associative/distributive (not the names!!)
Use the 4 operations to solve problems involving money and time.


Round positive whole numbers to the nearest 10,100,1000, and decimals to the nearest whole number or to 1d.p.

(BODMAS)

number > place value
