Stage 5

Number

Round numbers to the nearest integer, to a given power of ten, to one significant figure
and to 1 or 2 decimal places; estimate answers to problems involving decimals.
Use the terms square, positive square root, cube; use index notation for squares, cubes,
and powers of ten; recall the squares of 2 to 12 and cubes of 2, 3, 4, 5, and 10.
Understand equivalent fractions, simplifying a fraction by cancelling all common
factors; add, subtract and order fractions by writing them with a common denominator.
Use the equivalence between fractions, decimals and percentages in context including
performing division to convert a simple fraction to a decimal; solve problems involving
the calculation of fractions and percentages of quantities including cases where the
answer is a fraction.
Express one quantity as a fraction or percentage of another.


Algebra Solve simple linear equations in which the unknown appears on either side of the
equation.
Manipulate algebraic expressions by collecting like terms.
Generate terms of a sequence using term-to-term and position-to-term definitions of the
sequence; describe in words rules for linear sequences.		  
Shape

Convert measurements from one unit to another; convert between area measures,
including square centimetres and square metres; know rough metric equivalents of
pounds, feet, miles, pints and gallons.
Use and interpret maps and scale drawings, including 4 figure grid references and
estimating distances and areas; use bearings to specify direction.
Classify quadrilaterals by their geometric properties.

Explore the geometry of cuboids (including cubes) and shapes made from cuboids; find
the volumes of cuboids, recalling the formula; draw and interpret the net of a cuboid.
Analyse 3-D shapes through 2-D projections and cross-sections, including plans and
elevations.
Understand that translations are specified by combinations of horizontal and vertical
movements; translate triangles and other 2-D shapes.
Understand that rotations are specified by a centre and an angle; rotate triangles or other
2-D shapes about the origin; measure the angle of rotation using right angles and simple
fractions of a turn.

  
Data

List all outcomes for single events, and for two successive events, in a systematic way;
find probabilities.
Use and interpret the statistical measures mode, median, mean and range for discrete and
continuous data, including comparing distributions.
Construct and interpret pie charts.
