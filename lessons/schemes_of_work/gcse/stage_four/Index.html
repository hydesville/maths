Stage 4

Number

Solve problems involving all four operations on decimal numbers with up to three
decimal places, where the operation has to be determined.
Use decimal notation and recognise that each terminating decimal is a fraction; order
decimals; convert simple fractions of a whole to percentages of the whole and vice versa.
Use written methods to multiply and divide a 3-digit number by a 2-digit number.
Understand and use scales marked in ½, ¼, ?; perform additions and subtractions using
these fractions.
Understand the concepts and vocabulary of factor (divisor), multiple and common factor.
Solve simple ratio and proportion problems particularly in the context of recipes.
Solve problems using a range of skills including simple trial and improvement.


Algebra Solve problems involving substitution of positive numbers into simple algebraic
formulas.
Derive a simple formula.
Interpret information presented in a range of linear and non-linear graphs, including
travel (distance/time) graphs; calculate speed in simple cases.		  
Shape Recall and use properties of angles at a point, angles on a straight line, perpendicular
lines and opposite angles at a vertex; use angle properties of equilateral, isosceles and
right-angled triangles.
Find the area of a triangle; calculate perimeters and areas of shapes made from triangles
and rectangles.
Use axes and coordinates to specify or locate points in all four quadrants; find the
coordinates of points identified by geometrical information; find the midpoint of the line
segment AB, given points A and B.
Understand that reflections are specified by a mirror line; transform triangles and other
2-D shapes by reflection, using a line parallel to an axis.
Recognise and visualise rotation symmetry of 2-D shapes; identify the order of rotation
symmetry.		  
Data

Understand and use estimates and measures of probability; compare experimental data
and theoretical probabilities.
Use the range and measures of average for discrete data.
Interpret graphs representing real data, including recognising misleading diagrams.
